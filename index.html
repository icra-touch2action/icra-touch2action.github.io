From Touch to Action: Enhancing Robot Manipulation through Tactile Perception

Call for Submissions See Schedule

Why touch, why now?

Tactile sensing remains underexploited compared to vision or audition. This workshop takes a vertical view—from bioinspiration and sensor design to simulation, perception, and control—to make touch a first-class citizen in robot manipulation.

We combine keynote talks, an industry session, lightning talks, posters, and a concluding panel to map the road from foundational insights to deployable policies in the wild.

Workshop scope

Bioinspiration

Functional principles of human/animal touch shaping artificial tactile systems.

Transduction & Design

Magnetic, optical, capacitive; design trade-offs, materials, morphology, integration.

Simulation

From high-fidelity FEM to real-time simulators; closing the sim-to-real gap.

Perception & Touch Processing

Learning from tactile data; domain adaptation; multi-modal fusion with vision & proprioception.

Control for Manipulation

Model-based vs learning-based vs hybrid control; reliability in real deployments.
