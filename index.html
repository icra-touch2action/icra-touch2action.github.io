Call for Submissions See Schedule

Why touch, why now?

Tactile sensing remains underexploited compared to vision or audition. This workshop takes a vertical view—from bioinspiration and sensor design to simulation, perception, and control—to make touch a first-class citizen in robot manipulation.

We combine keynote talks, an industry session, lightning talks, posters, and a concluding panel to map the road from foundational insights to deployable policies in the wild.

Workshop scope

Bioinspiration

Functional principles of human and animal touch inspiring artificial tactile systems.

Transduction & Design

Exploring optical and magnetic sensing, design trade-offs, materials, morphology, and system integration.

Simulation

From high-fidelity FEM modeling to real-time simulators — bridging the sim-to-real gap in tactile sensing and control.

Perception & Touch Processing

Learning from tactile data — domain adaptation, and multimodal fusion with vision and proprioception.

Control for Manipulation

Enhancing multimodal control policies in imitation learning and reinforcement learning through tactile sensing.
